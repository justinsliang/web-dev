The Banana

Bananas are probably one of the greatest fruits on the planet.
They beat apples in almost every way, and is probably one of the most exquisite fruits to exist.

A Brief History

One of the more interesting quirks of the banana is how it actually grows on an herb, and the banana itself is actually a berry.
Another thing to not is that the banana we know today is not the banana that we first were introduced to. Throughout history, we have selectively bred and genetically modified
the banana in to becoming the fruit we know today, and for this reason bananas are not only a wonder of biology and human accomplishment, but also a serious biological problem, as
a serious disease like the Panama fungus may cause all dessert banana plants to die.

Some fun facts!

  • Bananas are very starchy which is why they make such great food for babies and the elderly
  • All of the bananas we usually see (the Cavendish) are biologically identical to each other
  • While the most common type of banana in non tropical areas is the dessert banana, plantains (bananas for cooking) are responsible for 85% of all banana production
